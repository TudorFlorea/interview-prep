## ðŸ“‹ Why This Question Matters

"Tell me about yourself" is typically the first question in behavioral interviews. It:
- Sets the tone for the entire conversation
- Gives you control over the narrative
- Highlights what you want the interviewer to remember
- Demonstrates communication skills

**Critical**: This is NOT your life story. It's a strategic career narrative.

## ðŸš« Common Mistakes to Avoid

### 1. The Chronological Resume Recitation
**Bad**:
> "I graduated in 2015, then I worked at Company A for 2 years doing X, then Company B for 3 years doing Y, then Company C..."

**Why It's Bad**: Boring, no narrative, sounds like reading your resume

**Fix**: Focus on themes and growth, not chronology

---

### 2. The Life Story
**Bad**:
> "I grew up in Ohio, went to State University where I majored in CS because I liked video games, had an internship at..."

**Why It's Bad**: Too much irrelevant detail, wastes time

**Fix**: Start with current role and work backwards selectively

---

### 3. The Technology List
**Bad**:
> "I know Python, Java, JavaScript, React, Node.js, PostgreSQL, MongoDB, Redis, Docker, Kubernetes, AWS..."

**Why It's Bad**: No context, doesn't show what you've actually built

**Fix**: Mention technologies in context of what you built with them

---

### 4. The Generic Answer
**Bad**:
> "I'm interested in your company because you do cool work with great people and I want to grow my career."

**Why It's Bad**: Could apply to any company, shows no research

**Fix**: Mention something specific about the company or role

---

### 5. Talking Too Long
**Bad**: 5+ minute monologue covering every detail

**Why It's Bad**: Loses interviewer's attention, eats interview time

**Fix**: Practice and time yourselfâ€”aim for 2-3 minutes

---

### 6. Being Too Humble
**Bad**:
> "I'm just a regular engineer doing normal work. I contribute to projects like everyone else."

**Why It's Bad**: Doesn't give interviewer anything to get excited about

**Fix**: Be specific about your impact without false modesty

---

### 7. Negativity About Current/Past Roles
**Bad**:
> "I'm looking to leave because my current company has terrible management and the code is a mess..."

**Why It's Bad**: Raises concerns you'll speak negatively about them next

**Fix**: Frame positivelyâ€”focus on what you're looking for, not what you're escaping

## ðŸŽ­ Practice Techniques

### 1. Write It Out First
- Draft your full answer (aim for ~300-400 words)
- Read it out loudâ€”does it sound natural?
- Time yourself reading it
- Revise until it flows naturally

### 2. Record Yourself
- Video or audio record your answer
- Watch/listen for:
  - Filler words (um, uh, like)
  - Pacing (too fast?)
  - Energy (enthusiastic?)
  - Body language (if video)

### 3. Get Feedback
- Practice with a friend or mentor
- Ask them:
  - What stood out to you?
  - What would you remember about me?
  - Did I talk too long?
  - Did any part confuse you?

### 4. Memorize Structure, Not Script
- Don't memorize word-for-word (sounds robotic)
- Memorize:
  - Key points in each section
  - Specific metrics
  - Transitions between sections
- Let the exact words vary each time

### 5. Practice Different Versions
- 2-minute version (standard)
- 1-minute version (if interrupted or time-constrained)
- 3-minute version (if they seem very interested)
