## üü† Amazon Leadership Principles (LPs)

Amazon has **16 Leadership Principles** that are deeply embedded in their interview process. Expect 4-6 behavioral questions, each targeting 1-2 LPs.

### Core Principles & Common Questions

#### 1. Customer Obsession
**Definition**: Leaders start with the customer and work backwards.

**What They Look For**:
- Prioritizing customer needs over internal preferences
- Using customer feedback to drive decisions
- Measuring success by customer impact

**Common Questions**:
- Tell me about a time you went above and beyond for a customer
- Describe a time when you had to balance customer needs with business constraints
- Give an example of when customer feedback changed your approach

**Story Approach**: Emphasize customer metrics, feedback loops, and customer-first thinking.

---

#### 2. Ownership
**Definition**: Leaders are owners. They think long term and don't sacrifice long-term value for short-term results.

**What They Look For**:
- Taking responsibility beyond your immediate role
- Thinking about long-term implications
- Acting on behalf of the entire company

**Common Questions**:
- Tell me about a time you took on something outside your area of responsibility
- Describe a time you made a decision that sacrificed short-term gains for long-term benefits
- Give an example of when you went beyond what was asked of you

**Story Approach**: Show initiative, long-term thinking, and willingness to own outcomes.

---

#### 3. Invent and Simplify
**Definition**: Leaders expect and require innovation and invention from their teams and always find ways to simplify.

**What They Look For**:
- Creative problem-solving
- Simplifying complex systems
- Questioning existing approaches

**Common Questions**:
- Tell me about a time you found a creative solution to a complex problem
- Describe a time when you simplified a process or system
- Give an example of when you challenged the status quo

**Story Approach**: Highlight innovation, automation, and reducing complexity.

---

#### 4. Are Right, A Lot
**Definition**: Leaders are right a lot. They have strong judgment and good instincts.

**What They Look For**:
- Data-driven decision making
- Learning from mistakes
- Seeking diverse perspectives

**Common Questions**:
- Tell me about a time you made a difficult decision with incomplete information
- Describe a time when your initial approach was wrong and how you corrected it
- Give an example of using data to make a decision

**Story Approach**: Show analytical thinking, adaptability, and learning from feedback.

---

#### 5. Learn and Be Curious
**Definition**: Leaders are never done learning and always seek to improve themselves.

**What They Look For**:
- Continuous learning
- Exploring new possibilities
- Intellectual curiosity

**Common Questions**:
- Tell me about a time you learned a new skill or technology
- Describe how you stay current with industry trends
- Give an example of when curiosity led you to a better solution

**Story Approach**: Demonstrate self-directed learning and applying new knowledge.

---

#### 6. Hire and Develop the Best
**Definition**: Leaders raise the performance bar with every hire and promotion.

**What They Look For**:
- Mentoring and coaching
- Recognizing and developing talent
- Raising team standards

**Common Questions**:
- Tell me about a time you mentored someone
- Describe how you've helped a struggling team member
- Give an example of raising the bar on your team

**Story Approach**: Focus on coaching, feedback, and improving team capabilities.

---

#### 7. Insist on the Highest Standards
**Definition**: Leaders have relentlessly high standards‚Äîmany people may think these standards are unreasonably high.

**What They Look For**:
- Quality focus
- Not settling for "good enough"
- Attention to detail

**Common Questions**:
- Tell me about a time you refused to compromise on quality
- Describe a time when you pushed back on a suboptimal solution
- Give an example of setting high standards for yourself or your team

**Story Approach**: Show commitment to excellence and quality over speed.

---

#### 8. Think Big
**Definition**: Thinking small is a self-fulfilling prophecy. Leaders create and communicate a bold direction.

**What They Look For**:
- Ambitious vision
- Thinking beyond immediate problems
- Inspiring others with big ideas

**Common Questions**:
- Tell me about a time you proposed a bold idea
- Describe your most ambitious project
- Give an example of thinking beyond the immediate solution

**Story Approach**: Highlight scope, vision, and long-term impact.

---

#### 9. Bias for Action
**Definition**: Speed matters in business. Many decisions are reversible and don't need extensive study.

**What They Look For**:
- Taking calculated risks
- Making decisions with incomplete information
- Moving quickly while managing risk

**Common Questions**:
- Tell me about a time you had to act quickly with limited information
- Describe a time when waiting would have been worse than acting
- Give an example of a calculated risk you took

**Story Approach**: Emphasize urgency, decisiveness, and iterative improvement.

---

#### 10. Frugality
**Definition**: Accomplish more with less. Constraints breed resourcefulness, self-sufficiency, and invention.

**What They Look For**:
- Resourcefulness
- Cost-consciousness
- Creative solutions with limited resources

**Common Questions**:
- Tell me about a time you delivered results with limited resources
- Describe how you've optimized costs
- Give an example of being resourceful

**Story Approach**: Show efficiency, cost savings, and doing more with less.

---

#### 11. Earn Trust
**Definition**: Leaders listen attentively, speak candidly, and treat others respectfully.

**What They Look For**:
- Transparency and honesty
- Admitting mistakes
- Building relationships

**Common Questions**:
- Tell me about a time you had to deliver bad news
- Describe a time when you built trust with a skeptical stakeholder
- Give an example of when you admitted a mistake

**Story Approach**: Show vulnerability, integrity, and relationship building.

---

#### 12. Dive Deep
**Definition**: Leaders operate at all levels, stay connected to the details, and audit frequently.

**What They Look For**:
- Attention to detail
- Understanding technical depth
- Hands-on problem-solving

**Common Questions**:
- Tell me about a time you debugged a complex issue
- Describe when you discovered a problem others missed
- Give an example of digging into details to find root cause

**Story Approach**: Show technical depth, thoroughness, and detailed understanding.

---

#### 13. Have Backbone; Disagree and Commit
**Definition**: Leaders are obligated to respectfully challenge decisions when they disagree. Once a decision is determined, they commit wholly.

**What They Look For**:
- Constructive disagreement
- Data-backed pushback
- Committing after decision is made

**Common Questions**:
- Tell me about a time you disagreed with a decision or approach
- Describe a time you had to commit to something you initially disagreed with
- Give an example of challenging someone senior to you

**Story Approach**: Show respectful disagreement followed by full commitment.

---

#### 14. Deliver Results
**Definition**: Leaders focus on the key inputs and deliver them with the right quality and in a timely fashion.

**What They Look For**:
- Execution and follow-through
- Overcoming obstacles
- Meeting commitments

**Common Questions**:
- Tell me about a time you had to meet a difficult deadline
- Describe a time when you overcame significant obstacles
- Give an example of delivering results under pressure

**Story Approach**: Emphasize outcomes, metrics, and overcoming challenges.

---

#### 15. Strive to be Earth's Best Employer
**Definition**: Leaders work every day to create a safer, more productive, higher performing, more diverse, and more just work environment.

**What They Look For**:
- Creating inclusive environments
- Employee development
- Work-life harmony

**Common Questions**:
- Tell me about a time you improved team morale or culture
- Describe how you've supported diversity and inclusion
- Give an example of helping create a better work environment

**Story Approach**: Focus on team well-being, inclusion, and positive culture.

---

#### 16. Success and Scale Bring Broad Responsibility
**Definition**: We started in a garage, but we're not there anymore. Leaders create more than they consume and leave things better than they found them.

**What They Look For**:
- Considering broader impact
- Sustainability thinking
- Leaving things better

**Common Questions**:
- Tell me about a time you considered the broader impact of your work
- Describe how you've left a team or system better than you found it
- Give an example of thinking beyond immediate business needs

**Story Approach**: Show long-term thinking and positive legacy.

---

### Amazon Interview Tips

1. **Use LP Language**: Mention the principle by name in your answer ("This demonstrates bias for action...")
2. **Expect Follow-Ups**: Be ready for deep dives like "What would you do differently?" and "Why did you make that choice?"
3. **Quantify Everything**: Amazon loves metrics and data
4. **Show Trade-Offs**: Discuss alternatives you considered
5. **Prepare 2 stories per LP**: Have backups ready

## üîµ Google - Googleyness & General Cognitive Ability

Google evaluates **4 areas**: Role-Related Knowledge, General Cognitive Ability, Leadership, and **Googleyness** (cultural fit).

### Googleyness Attributes

#### 1. Ambiguity Handling
**Definition**: Comfort with ambiguity and ability to thrive in undefined situations.

**What They Look For**:
- Navigating unclear requirements
- Asking clarifying questions
- Making progress despite uncertainty

**Common Questions**:
- Tell me about a time you had to work with vague requirements
- Describe a project where the goal kept changing
- How do you approach problems without clear solutions?

---

#### 2. Collaboration
**Definition**: Working effectively with diverse teams and perspectives.

**What They Look For**:
- Cross-functional partnership
- Seeking input from others
- Team success over individual glory

**Common Questions**:
- Describe a time you worked with a difficult team member
- Tell me about collaborating across teams
- Give an example of putting team goals first

---

#### 3. Intellectual Humility
**Definition**: Willingness to learn, admit mistakes, and change your mind with new data.

**What They Look For**:
- Openness to feedback
- Admitting when wrong
- Learning from failures

**Common Questions**:
- Tell me about a time you changed your mind based on new information
- Describe a failure and what you learned
- Give an example of receiving difficult feedback

---

#### 4. Growth Mindset
**Definition**: Believing abilities can be developed through dedication and hard work.

**What They Look For**:
- Continuous improvement
- Learning from challenges
- Embracing difficult problems

**Common Questions**:
- How do you approach learning new technologies?
- Describe overcoming a skill gap
- Tell me about tackling something outside your comfort zone

---

#### 5. Conscientiousness
**Definition**: Being thorough, careful, and dependable in your work.

**What They Look For**:
- Attention to detail
- Following through on commitments
- Quality focus

**Common Questions**:
- Tell me about ensuring quality in your work
- Describe managing multiple priorities
- Give an example of preventing a potential issue

---

### Google Interview Tips

1. **Show Your Thinking**: Talk through your problem-solving process
2. **Collaborative Tone**: Use "we" when discussing team work, "I" for your specific contributions
3. **Data-Driven**: Back up claims with metrics
4. **Demonstrate Learning**: Show how you grow from experiences
5. **Be Humble**: Acknowledge others' contributions

## üü¶ Microsoft - Growth Mindset & Core Values

Microsoft transformed under Satya Nadella's leadership, emphasizing **growth mindset** and collaboration.

### Core Values

#### 1. Growth Mindset
**Definition**: Belief that abilities and intelligence can be developed.

**What They Look For**:
- Learning from failures
- Embracing challenges
- Persisting through setbacks
- Seeking feedback

**Common Questions**:
- Tell me about a time you failed and what you learned
- Describe how you approach learning new skills
- Give an example of receiving critical feedback

**Story Approach**: Emphasize learning, adaptation, and continuous improvement.

---

#### 2. Customer Empathy
**Definition**: Understanding and caring about customer needs and experiences.

**What They Look For**:
- User-centric thinking
- Empathy in design decisions
- Accessibility considerations

**Common Questions**:
- Describe a time you advocated for the user
- Tell me about balancing technical constraints with user needs
- Give an example of improving user experience

---

#### 3. Diversity & Inclusion
**Definition**: Creating an environment where everyone can do their best work.

**What They Look For**:
- Inclusive practices
- Valuing different perspectives
- Building diverse teams

**Common Questions**:
- How do you ensure diverse perspectives are heard?
- Describe working with someone very different from you
- Tell me about creating an inclusive environment

---

#### 4. One Microsoft
**Definition**: Collaboration across teams and organizations.

**What They Look For**:
- Breaking down silos
- Cross-team collaboration
- Shared success mentality

**Common Questions**:
- Describe collaborating with another team
- Tell me about a time you helped another team succeed
- Give an example of cross-functional work

---

#### 5. Making a Difference
**Definition**: Focus on impact that matters to customers, partners, and the world.

**What They Look For**:
- Meaningful impact
- Social responsibility
- Long-term thinking

**Common Questions**:
- What impact do you want your work to have?
- Describe a project you're most proud of
- Tell me about work that made a real difference

---

### Microsoft Interview Tips

1. **Emphasize Learning**: Show how you learn from failures
2. **Collaboration Focus**: Highlight cross-team work
3. **Humility**: Acknowledge contributions from others
4. **Impact**: Connect work to customer outcomes
5. **Inclusivity**: Mention diverse perspectives and inclusive practices
